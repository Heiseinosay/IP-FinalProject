Leg Extensions

Leg extensions are exercises usually done with a lever machine. You sit on a padded seat and raise a padded bar with your legs.

3 sets of 6 to 8 repetitions
Target Muscle:
Instructions:
1. Place your hands on the hand bars.
2. Lift the weight while exhaling until your legs are almost straight. Do not lock your knees. Keep your back against the backrest and do not arch your back.
3. Exhale and lower the weight back to starting position. Repeat.
Benefits: The difference is that in the squat, the body part you're exercising is anchored, while in the leg extension, you're moving the padded bar, which means your legs aren't stationary as they work, and thus the chain of movement is open in the leg extension.
