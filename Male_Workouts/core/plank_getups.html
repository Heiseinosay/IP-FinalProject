Plank Get Ups

The Plank Get Ups â€” also known as the "up down planks" is the combination of the two exercises (Plank and Push-up), that provide the benefits of both.

3 sets of 10 to 12 repetitions
Target Muscle:
Instructions:
1. Lie face down on an exercise mat with hands should be shoulder-width apart; palms flat on the floor.
2. Get up in a push up position, bend your elbows and rest your weight on your forearms. Your body should form a straight line from your shoulders to your ankles.
3. Brace your core by contracting your abs as if you were about to be punched in the gut. Press your body up into the top position of a push-up by extending your arms one at a time. Repeat.
Benefits: The up and down plank strengthens and tones your core, glutes, arms, wrists, and shoulders. This exercise helps to improve your posture, tightens the midsection, and boosts weight loss.
